How to use Vendor Credit Claims

A guide to running a promotion from setup through to recovering the vendor credit.

What this tool does

It replaces the per-promo Excel template and the email hand-offs between teams. For each promotion you track, in one place:

  • The promo & its lines — products, RRP, discount, expected sales, per-retailer.
  • Inbound customer claims — what each retailer claims back for the discount they gave.
  • The outbound vendor request — the credit MacGear claims back from the brand.

The discount is funded three ways — Retailer / MacGear / Brand — so claims aren't 1:1: MacGear absorbs its share. The calculations match the Excel template exactly.

Quick start — run a promo end to end

  1. Create the promotion. Click + New Promotion. Fill in the claim #, name, brand, date range, the AUD→USD rate, and the default funding split. Tick the retailers taking part (set each one's rebate if it differs).
  2. Add line items. On the promo page, add SKUs under each retailer. Type the product code and the description / RRP / buy price autofill from the product master. Enter the % off and the 6-week average weekly units. You can copy SKUs from another retailer block to save re-keying.
  3. Send for approval. Move the promo status through the workflow (see below) as the vendor approves the spend and Sales releases it to the customer.
  4. Log the customer claim. After the promo runs, enter each retailer's actual sales and the amount claimed, and attach their claim document.
  5. Raise the vendor request. The vendor (brand) claim is calculated for you; send it to the vendor and track it through to Credit Received.

The line-item columns

  • 6wk avg — the customer's average weekly unit sales over the prior 6 weeks. Used (with growth) to project expected sales over the promo period.
  • Actual sales — the real units sold once known. The customer-claim summary shows both an expected figure (from 6wk avg) and an actual figure (from this). The quantity highlights green when actuals beat expectation and red when they fall short.
  • RRP / % Off / Promo Price — the last three columns. Promo price is simply RRP less the % off. In the line editor you can type either the % off or the promo price and the other fills in automatically.
  • Support basis — how each line's support is calculated:
    • % off (discount) — the default. Support = net buy × % off × funding ratio. The promo margin is an output.
    • Target promo margin — you enter a target end-to-end promo margin and the support is back-solved to hit it (the % off still sets the customer promo price). Lines on this basis show a ⌖ marker next to their % off.
  • Funding split — each line uses the promo's default split unless you tick override on that line. The three shares must total 100% — the retailer share fills in automatically as the remainder and a line won't save unless it balances.

The three views

Every promo can be shown three ways using the tabs at the top of the promo page — the same data, framed for different audiences. Each view also has an Excel export:

  • Internal — the full picture, including MacGear's absorbed share.
  • Sales — the version to share with the customer/Sales team.
  • Vendor — the version supporting the credit request to the brand.

The dashboard

The home page lists every promo with its current statuses. The tiles at the top summarise the set you're looking at — active promos, total customer claimed, vendor amount still to recover, vendor credits received, and what MacGear has absorbed. Use the brand and status filters to narrow the list; the tiles update to match.

Attachments

You can upload a file against each retailer — typically the claim document that accompanies their request. Files are stored with the promo and can be downloaded or removed later.

Good to know

  • Money is AUD, ex-GST unless a field is explicitly labelled USD. RRP is shown GST-inclusive (the GST factor is 1.1).
  • % off is entered as a percent (e.g. 15 for 15%).
  • Editing the promo header — name, claim #, dates and the AUD→USD rate can be changed after creation from the promo page.
  • Dark mode — use the 🌙 button in the top bar; your choice is remembered on this browser.
